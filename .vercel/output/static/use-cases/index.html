Data Intelligence Use Cases

Marketing team spending senior time on research that should be automated

The Situation

A marketing team of four is running account-based campaigns. Before any campaign goes live, someone — usually a senior person — spends hours building and cleaning the target list. By the time the list is ready, some of it is already out of date.

What We Built

A continuous data pipeline that sources, enriches, and qualifies target accounts automatically — filtered by ICP criteria the team defined once, updated on an ongoing basis. Campaign prep that used to take two days now takes thirty minutes.

The Outcome

Senior time redirected to strategy and execution. Data quality improved. Campaign velocity increased. The research backlog eliminated.

HR team mapping a talent market manually every quarter

The Situation

A growing company's HR team conducts quarterly talent market reviews — identifying candidates, tracking competitor hiring, and building briefing documents for leadership. The process is entirely manual. Two people spend the better part of a week on it each quarter.

What We Built

An automated research and monitoring system that tracks the talent market continuously. An agentic workflow summarizes findings in the exact format the HR team uses for leadership briefings, updated weekly rather than quarterly.

The Outcome

The quarterly manual process was eliminated. Leadership receives better intelligence more frequently. The two people who ran the process are now doing work that actually requires their judgment.

Growth team making decisions on data nobody trusts

The Situation

A B2B SaaS company's growth team uses CRM data to prioritize accounts, segment outreach, and allocate resources. The problem: nobody fully trusts the data. Fields are incomplete. Records are duplicated. Enrichment was done once, two years ago.

What We Built

A data cleaning, normalization, and enrichment pipeline that ran as a one-time remediation project, followed by an ongoing refresh system. Every account in the CRM now has complete, current enrichment data.

The Outcome

Segmentation accuracy improved significantly. Outreach became more targeted. Wasted effort on wrong-fit accounts dropped. The "I don't trust the data" conversation stopped happening.

Sales Acceleration Use Cases

Outbound team producing inconsistent results despite high activity

The Situation

A five-person sales team is sending outbound every week. High volume. Low results. Investigation reveals: lists are sourced differently by each rep, personalization varies wildly, and follow-up cadences are inconsistent.

What We Built

A unified outbound system using Clay as the data backbone. One targeting logic, applied consistently. Enrichment waterfalls that pull the right context. Sequences that run on schedule regardless of whether someone remembered.

The Outcome

Outreach quality became consistent. Reply rates improved. The team now runs 3x the volume with better results and less manual effort per rep.

Founder doing outbound personally because "nobody else can do it right"

The Situation

A founder at a 20-person B2B services firm is running outbound themselves. They're good at it. They also shouldn't be doing it. Their approach is intuitive, undocumented, and impossible to delegate.

What We Built

We spent time understanding how the founder thinks about targeting and personalization — then systematized it. The ICP logic was codified into Clay. The research signals were automated. The message framework was documented and built into sequences.

The Outcome

The founder stepped out of outbound entirely within six weeks. The system now runs what they used to run — at higher volume, with consistent quality, without their time.

Sales team getting meetings but losing them to slow follow-up

The Situation

A SaaS company's inbound leads were converting to first meetings at a reasonable rate. After the first meeting, follow-up was slow, inconsistent, and manual. Some deals went cold because nobody followed up in time.

What We Built

A post-meeting follow-up system triggered automatically — with enriched context from the CRM, personalized sequences based on meeting outcome, and task creation for reps when human judgment was genuinely required.

The Outcome

Pipeline leakage from slow follow-up dropped significantly. Reps spent less time on administrative follow-up and more on active opportunities. Several deals were recovered in the first month.
